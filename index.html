Debit Re-Routing Technology

Routing intelligence that
pays for itself

New Federal Reserve guidance (Regulation II) now requires that card-not-present debit transactions support at least two unaffiliated networks — opening the door to route over cheaper regional rails instead of Visa or Mastercard. Corduro built the technology to capture every dollar of that savings.

01
Real-Time Network Analysis
Continuously monitors cost, speed, and reliability across STAR, NYCE, Accel, Pulse, SHAZAM, and CU24 simultaneously.
02
Least-Cost Path Selection
Proprietary decision engine selects the lowest-cost Durbin-compliant network route in milliseconds — automatically, on every transaction.
03
Automatic Failover
If any network degrades, traffic instantly re-routes with zero merchant-facing impact and full settlement continuity.

Regulatory Tailwind

The law is now
on your side

A fundamental shift in U.S. payment regulation is opening trillions in debit volume to competition. Corduro is the rare processor with the technology and network relationships to capture it.

"Tokenization = network lock-in disguised as security."

National Retail Federation & FTC — on Visa/MC's anticompetitive practices
Federal Reserve · Regulation II
CNP Debit Now Requires Two Unaffiliated Networks
New Fed guidance mandates that card-not-present debit transactions must support at least two unaffiliated networks — forcing Visa and Mastercard to compete with regional rails for the first time in CNP.
FTC Action
Mastercard Forced to Allow De-Tokenization
The FTC compelled Mastercard to allow competitor networks to de-tokenize transactions — breaking the "tokenization barricade" that had forced merchants onto higher-cost Visa/MC rails.
DOJ · Active Investigation
DOJ Files Antitrust Suit Against Visa
The Department of Justice filed a lawsuit against Visa for monopolizing debit markets through anticompetitive tokenization practices — further reopening CNP routing competition across the industry.
Market Signal
Amazon & Walmart Already Rerouting
The largest online merchants deliberately avoided network tokenization and route debit to regional networks — saving 15–30 bps per transaction. Corduro makes this capability available to every merchant.
